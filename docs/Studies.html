Studies

Seattle Postpartum Health Study

As co-principal investigator of the Seattle Postpartum Health Study, I was directly responsible for every aspect of the project, including the following:

  1. Study design
  2. Grant writing (funded by NSF and Wenner-Gren Foundation)
  3. IRB approval
  4. Informed consent
  5. Data management plan
  6. Participant recruitment
  7. Data collection (including biospecimens)
  8. Research personnel management
  9. Wet lab work
  10. Data analysis and manuscript preparation (in prep)

Tsimane Health and Life History Project

As a graduate researcher for the Tsimane Health and Life History Project, I utilized pre-existing data to conduct a series of computational studies looking at how ecological conditions (e.g., parasite burden) drive population-level differences in immune function across reproductive states (e.g., pregnancy). This involved:

  1. Study design
  2. Grant writing (funding by the NSF)
  3. Extensive data wrangling / cleaning
  4. Data analysis
  5. Manuscript preparation (see Publications, Statistics)

Incline Study

As a Post Baccalaureate Research Fellow, I worked with Dr Cara Wall-Scheffler to collect energetic and thermoregulation data for a study investigating the sex-specific effects of terrain on optimal walking speed and cost of transport (results published here). My responsibilities included:

  1. Participant recruitment
  2. Data collection
  3. Dissemination of results

Humor and Gender Study

As an undergraduate researcher, this was my first experience conducting research. Under the mentorship of Dr Cara Wall-Scheffler, I designed and executed a study investigating how comedian gender impacts audience response to jokes. My responsibilities included:

  1. Study design
  2. IRB approval (study included deception, thus requiring a full in-person committee review)
  3. Participant recruitment (final n=89)
  4. Data collection
  5. Data analysis
  6. Dissemination of results
